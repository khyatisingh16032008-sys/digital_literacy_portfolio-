## Reflection (150–200 words)

For Task 3, I used two platforms: HackerRank for coding practice and Google Forms
for collaboration. Both tools are extremely valuable for a computer science student
and will be part of my regular academic workflow.

HackerRank introduced me to the concept of online judges — platforms that
automatically test your code against multiple input cases. Even completing a simple
"Hello, World!" challenge taught me the submission process, which prepares me for
more complex problems later. I can see myself using HackerRank regularly to practice
Data Structures and Algorithms as I progress through my B.Tech.

Google Forms was a completely new experience. I created a 5-question Digital Literacy
Awareness Quiz that could be sent to my batchmates to test their knowledge. I found
it surprisingly easy to design good quiz questions. The platform automatically collects
responses in a Google Sheet, which makes analysis simple.

Both tools are free, browser-based, and require no installation — exactly the type
of accessible technology that every student should know. These skills will help me
academically in conducting surveys, taking part in coding contests, and collaborating
with teams remotely.
