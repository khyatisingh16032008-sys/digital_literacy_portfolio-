# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

## DO's

| # | Do |
|---|----|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional.** Use a clear, appropriate profile photo and write a bio that reflects your academic and professional interests. |
| 2 | **Think before you post.** Ask yourself: Would I be comfortable if my professor, a future employer, or my parents saw this? If no, do not post it. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings.** Regularly review who can see your posts, photos, and personal information on all platforms. |
| 4 | **Give credit when sharing.** If you share someone else's article, image, or idea, always tag the original creator or cite the source. |
| 5 | **Engage positively and constructively.** If you disagree with someone online, express your view respectfully with facts and reasoning. |
| 6 | **Report harmful content.** If you see bullying, hate speech, or misinformation, use the platform's reporting tools rather than engaging with it. |

## DON'TS

| # | Don't |
|---|--------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal or financial information publicly.** Never post your phone number, address, Aadhaar number, or bank details on any social platform. |
| 2 | **Don't engage with or forward unverified news.** Check facts on credible sources (PIB Fact Check, Reuters, etc.) before sharing news that seems surprising or alarming. |
| 3 | **Don't post in anger or frustration.** Emotional posts made in the heat of the moment can cause lasting reputational damage — wait 24 hours before posting about a conflict. |
| 4 | **Don't overshare your real-time location.** Avoid posting live locations, tagging yourself at your hostel or home, or sharing travel plans publicly. |
| 5 | **Don't respond to strangers asking for personal help or money.** Scammers often pose as peers, professors, or well-wishers on social platforms. |
